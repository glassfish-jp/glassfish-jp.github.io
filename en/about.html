About the Group

Home > About

GlassFish Users Group Japan is a Java User Group (JUG) that is consisted by Jakarta EE developers and users in Japan. We are a member of Java Community Process and specify for Jakarta EE and relative technologies.

Everyone are main actor in the group however each roles are different. We all create the future of the community.

Activities

  • Planning and holding events that offline discussion about Jakarta EE and relative technologies in Japan.
  • Online discussion using mailing list and technology forum.
  • Co-operation with other JUGs.

History

Establishment

This group is established in 2006 by techonology evangelists of Sun Microsystems Japan. The first president was Takayuki Okazaki, and Yoshio Terada joined as a board member.

At first, the group was led by a vendor (Sun Microsystems). The main mission of the group was introduction to GlassFish (reference implementation of Java EE) for users and developers in Japan.

With Sun Microsystems

We acted in "Hot Topic Seminor" by Sun Microsystems and several conferences. Some core members also acted as contributors for GlassFish I18N project.

With Oracle Japan

Sun Microsystems was acquired by Oracle and the Japanese company became a part of Oracle Japan on June 2010. The group restarted with support of Oracle Japan. Kenji Hasunuma joined as the vice-president and acted over many works of the president. After that, the group had acted to introduce Java EE and GlassFish in Japan until September 2015.

Become a vendor dependent JUG

The group was independent from support of Oracle Japan on September 2015. Takayuki Okazaki (president) and Kenji Hasunuma (vice-president) resigned at the time, and several staffs have been leading the group. Currently, the group is building system that all the member can each members act their own role.
